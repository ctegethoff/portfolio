Learning

Users set up and watch live TV in fundamentally different ways around the world.

Context

Roku's expansion into international markets marked a major shift—requiring the OS to scale across languages, countries, norms, and market expectations while maintaining its core promise of ease of use.

Problem / Opportunity

How might we design an intuitive, accessible TV OS for a global audience? How can we make it work for diverse languages, cultures, and varying levels of tech familiarity?

Contribution

Taking a systems design approach to the OS, I helped improve the product's modularity and accessibility, ensuring it was scalable and adaptable to diverse user needs. This also drove consistency across the OS. See how Roku outperforms the competition.

Impact

In 2022, Roku was the #1 smart TV OS. Roku saw a 14% year-over-year increase in active accounts to 61.3 million in Q1 2022.

Applied UX Laws*

Selective Attention

The process of focusing our attention only to a subset of the stimuli in the environment — usually those related to our goals.

Hick's Law

The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices.
