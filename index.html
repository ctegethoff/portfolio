I never planned to be a UX designer. Years ago, I was finishing a Bachelor of Science in Industrial Design at The Ohio State University when everything shifted during a solo trip through Europe.

With no idea where I was, no money, no phone, and no way to speak the language, I sat on a bench in a train station and people watched to kill time. Trains arrived and departed. People came and went. The longer I observed, the more patterns emerged. I started to feel like I could step into other people's perspectives, which felt strangely powerful and exciting.

That was when the idea for my senior thesis finally clicked. The project itself isn’t important, but it combined a user interface with a physical product. NCR saw past my average industrial design skills and brought me onto a small team as one of their first UI designers, working on early multimodal interactions. I didn’t even know what a UI designer was. That’s how I stumbled into UX.

Since then, I’ve built a career, or really multiple careers, designing clear, user focused experiences across TV, desktop, mobile, and emerging platforms. I’ve worked with innovation teams at Google, Microsoft, Intel, Nike, and NCR to turn complex technology into products people can actually use.

I’m drawn to clarity, structure, and the messy middle where problems live. Whether working independently or on a team, I lead with curiosity, rely on research, and keep the user at the center of everything I build.
